# Microsoft Teams

Microsoft designed Teams **to allow external guests** to attend meetings 
with **zero software installation and no account login**.

## How It Works as a Guest

* Pure Web App: The entire meeting runs directly inside your web browser (Google Chrome or Microsoft Edge are recommended for full feature support).
* Instant Access: You bypass the Microsoft login screen completely by choosing the anonymous guest path.
* Privacy Guard: The prospect cannot see your personal Microsoft account details, calendar, or files because you are not logged in.

## The Exact 3-Click Workflow

   1. Click the meeting link in your email invite.
   2. Click the button that says "Continue on this browser" when the webpage opens.
   3. Type any display name you want the prospect to see, then click "Join now".

## Crucial Caveats to Keep in Mind

* The Lobby: Because you are unauthenticated, you will hit a virtual waiting room. The host must click "Admit" to let you into the call.
* Browser Limits: Firefox and Safari sometimes block microphone or camera permissions in Teams web. Stick to Chrome or Edge to avoid pre-meeting tech panic.
* Feature Loss: As a web guest, you cannot use custom virtual backgrounds (only background blur is available) or upload files directly into the meeting chat.

# Microphone

Because you are joining anonymously through a web browser, Microsoft Teams' built-in automated "Test Call" bot will not be available to you. However, you can easily test your microphone and check the visual audio meter using the pre-meeting screen right before you let the prospect know you have arrived. [1, 2, 3, 4] 

## Phase 1: Test Your Mic in Windows 11 Pro First

Ensure your hardware is active at the operating system level before launching the browser. [5] 

   1. Press the Windows Key + I to open the Windows 11 Settings menu.
   2. Select System from the sidebar, then click Sound.
   3. Scroll down to the Input section and click on your active microphone.
   4. Speak out loud. Watch the blue "Test your microphone" volume bar bounce. If it moves, Windows is successfully capturing your voice. [5, 6] 

## Phase 2: Test on the Teams Pre-Meeting Screen

Use the meeting link early to verify your web permissions and check Teams' live audio meter. [5, 7] 

   1. Click the prospect's invite link and choose "Continue on this browser".
   2. Look at the pop-up near your browser's address bar and click Allow to give Teams permission to use your microphone.
   3. Toggle the Video / Camera icon to OFF so you do not stream your webcam.
   4. Toggle the Microphone icon to ON.
   5. Click the Gear icon (or "Open device settings") located below the join preview window.
   6. Verify your preferred microphone is selected in the dropdown menu.
   7. Speak a few words. You will see a live audio indicator / wave animation moving next to the microphone icon. If that visual indicator bounces as you speak, the web browser is sending your audio perfectly. [4, 5, 7, 8, 9] 

Once you see the audio meter moving, you can confidently type your name and click "Join now". [4, 10] 
If your audio bar does not move during these steps, let me know if you are using an external headset or your laptop's built-in mic so we can check your browser privacy blocks.
